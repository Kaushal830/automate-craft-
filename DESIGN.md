# AutomateCraft Design System

## Brand & Style

AutomateCraft presents itself as a premium AI automation workspace, not a conventional SaaS dashboard. The visual identity is anchored in **deep graphite blacks**, **cold glass surfaces**, and a **single electric-blue accent** that signals intelligence, focus, and activation. The overall mood is confident, technical, and cinematic, but never loud.

The interface should feel like a control room for AI-assisted work:

- Public-facing pages are sparse, dramatic, and centered around the prompt composer.
- Authenticated product surfaces feel denser and more tool-like, using layered panels, side navigation, and structured chat output.
- Visual richness comes from depth, blur, edge-light, and hierarchy rather than from bright colors or decorative illustration.

The wordmark should read as primarily neutral, with **"Craft"** carrying the accent color. That blue is a signal color, not a general fill color. Most of the product should remain black, charcoal, silver, and glass-gray.

## Color System

The palette is intentionally restrained. Nearly every surface belongs to one of four roles:

1. **Base blacks** for page canvas and app shell
2. **Graphite panels** for structure and content grouping
3. **Soft glass overlays** for premium depth
4. **Electric blue** for interaction, focus, active state, and AI emphasis

Pure black is avoided as the only background. Instead, the product uses layered blacks (`#09090b`, `#08090b`, `#0f1115`) so the interface feels deep rather than empty.

Blue should be used in these situations:

- primary actions
- active navigation
- focused inputs
- highlighted text within key statements
- subtle atmospheric glows behind hero or chat focal points

Blue should **not** be used as a blanket border color across inactive elements. Inactive chrome stays in low-contrast white alpha values.

## Typography

Inter is the sole typeface and should remain the entire system.

The typography hierarchy is defined by contrast in **scale**, **weight**, and **opacity**, not by introducing multiple fonts. Headlines should feel compressed and editorial, with tight letter-spacing and short line-height. Supporting text should relax into generous line-height and reduced opacity.

### Typographic behavior

- Large hero and intro headlines use very tight tracking and near-solid white.
- Supporting copy is intentionally softer, typically between 40% and 55% white.
- Labels, metadata, and eyebrow text rely on uppercase spacing rather than bold size jumps.
- Product UI text should feel calm and utility-driven, never marketing-heavy once inside the app.

## Layout & Composition

The product has two major compositional modes:

### 1. Public mode

The public experience is centered, spacious, and composed like a premium AI launch surface. The prompt composer is the visual anchor. Supporting sections remain dark and spacious, using stat strips, compact cards, and restrained motion.

### 2. Product mode

The authenticated workspace becomes more architectural:

- fixed or anchored left navigation
- central working canvas
- layered cards and floating controls
- top-right utility actions

The visual system should preserve breathing room even when density increases. Content should not feel cramped. Cards are used to organize and clarify, but the UI should still feel atmospheric rather than grid-heavy.

## Surfaces & Depth

Depth is one of the system's strongest traits. It should be created through:

- tonal layering
- soft shadows
- white-alpha borders
- glass blur
- subtle blue ambient haze

There are three primary surface behaviors:

### Standard cards

Use dark graphite gradients with a subtle top-to-bottom tonal shift. Standard cards should feel solid and structural.

### Glass cards

Use translucent black surfaces with blur and a faint white edge. These are best for overlays, chat panels, dropdowns, and premium utility layers.

### Accent focus layers

Use carefully controlled blue glow only when something is active, selected, or primary. This glow should feel like energy under the surface, not neon in front of it.

## Core Components

### Prompt composer

The prompt composer is the visual and emotional centerpiece of the brand. It should feel like a tool, not a form:

- dark charcoal fill
- white input text
- low-contrast border at rest
- stronger blue border on focus
- soft halo glow when active
- rounded rectangle shape, not pill shape

It should always read as the most important object on the public home and remain premium inside chat/product contexts.

### Navigation

Navigation bars and top utility shells use glass-dark panels with strong blur, subtle outlines, and capsule geometry. Active states use blue sparingly. Navigation should feel high-precision and polished, not playful.

### Sidebar

The sidebar belongs to the app shell, not the marketing site. It should feel matte and structural. Active rows can carry a blue glow or left-edge cue, but inactive rows stay quiet and monochrome.

### Chat messages

The chat system is not a playful messenger. It is a structured AI workspace:

- user messages may carry a blue-tinted elevated treatment
- system and AI messages should remain glass-dark and readable
- inline cards should look like product modules, not generic chat bubbles

### Buttons

Primary buttons use the brand blue gradient and pill geometry. Secondary buttons stay neutral, dark, and glassy. Buttons should feel pressable and premium, with hover movement that is subtle and vertical rather than flashy.

## Motion

Motion should reinforce intelligence and polish.

The system uses calm movement with smooth ease curves. Hover effects should feel buoyant, not bouncy. Entrance motion should be brief and precise. Ambient animation may exist in backgrounds, particles, or typewriter behavior, but it should never dominate the user's attention.

### Motion principles

- Use lift and glow, not spin and bounce.
- Favor fades, short upward movement, and slight scale.
- Maintain reduced-motion compliance everywhere.
- Let the prompt composer and active buttons feel most alive.

### Typical motion roles

- Navigation: quiet entrance and underline growth
- Cards: soft lift with a stronger shadow
- Buttons: short upward or scale movement
- Backgrounds: slow atmospheric drift
- Chat/workspace: subtle state changes, not theatrical transitions

## Spacing

Spacing is generous and intentional. The system should feel premium by leaving room around important objects, especially headlines, prompt composers, cards, and grouped controls.

- Public sections should breathe with large vertical spacing.
- Product surfaces may be denser, but never cramped.
- Small text clusters should use rhythm through opacity and line-height rather than by stacking too tightly.

The 8px grid should govern the system, but the emotional feel should be **cinematic precision**, not rigid corporate alignment.

## Accessibility

Even with the dark cinematic styling, readability must remain strong:

- primary text should stay very high contrast
- secondary text should remain readable on graphite, not fade into it
- focus states must always be visible
- blue glows should support focus, not replace clear outlines
- reduced-motion mode should disable ambient flourish while preserving hierarchy

Glass and blur should never compromise legibility. When in doubt, increase text clarity and reduce visual effects.

## Design Intent Summary

AutomateCraft should feel like:

- a premium AI operator console
- serious, modern, and composed
- cinematic but useful
- dark by default, with electric blue used as a precision instrument

It should not feel like:

- a generic black dashboard
- a neon hacker interface
- a flat enterprise admin template
- a bright marketing SaaS

The finished identity is best described as **graphite intelligence with electric focus**: dark, confident, structured, and quietly alive.
